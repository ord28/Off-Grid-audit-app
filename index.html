How Solar Panels Work

Solar panels convert sunlight into DC electricity through photovoltaic cells. Multiple panels form an array that feeds your inverter, which converts DC to AC for your appliances. Peak generation happens between 10am–3pm when the sun is highest.

Pro Tip
Clean your panels monthly with a soft cloth and water. Dust and bird droppings can reduce output by up to 25%.
🔋
Battery Care & Maintenance

Lithium (LiFePO4) batteries are low maintenance but should stay between 20–80% charge for longest life. Never fully discharge them. Lead-acid batteries need monthly distilled water top-ups and should never sit discharged — this causes permanent sulfation damage.

Battery Life
Lithium: 3,000–6,000 cycles · Lead-Acid: 300–1,200 cycles · Avoid temperatures above 40°C.
⚡
Understanding Your Inverter

The inverter is the brain of your system. A hybrid inverter manages solar input, battery charging, grid fallback and AC output simultaneously. Check the display weekly — error codes like F01 or E07 indicate faults. Keep it in a cool, ventilated space away from direct sunlight.

Important
Never overload your inverter. Keep total load below 80% of its rated capacity for longevity and efficiency.
📊
Load Management Tips

Stagger heavy appliances — don't run the AC, washing machine and iron simultaneously. Use high-wattage devices (iron, kettle, microwave) during peak solar hours (10am–3pm) so panels power them directly without draining batteries. LED lights use 80% less power than incandescent.

Energy Saving
Replace a standard 1.5hp AC with an inverter-type AC and cut energy consumption by up to 50%.
🔌
Wiring & Safety

DC wiring from panels carries high voltage — never touch or modify without a qualified technician. Install surge protectors on both AC and DC sides. MC4 connectors must be properly locked — a loose connector causes arcing and fire risk. Ensure all breakers are correctly rated and accessible.

Safety First
Install a DC isolator switch between panels and inverter. Never work on live circuits without isolation.
🗓️
Maintenance Schedule
Weekly Check inverter display, confirm battery charge level is healthy
Monthly Clean panels, check cable connections, inspect for corrosion
6 Months Torque-check all terminals, test bypass changeover, log battery voltage
Yearly Professional inspection, battery capacity test, IR scan of panels

Common Questions
Why does my inverter beep?
Continuous beeping usually means low battery or overload. Short beeps on switching are normal. Check your inverter manual for specific error codes.
Why isn't my battery charging?
Check if panels are shaded or dirty. Verify MPPT settings match your battery type. A tripped DC breaker also stops charging completely.
How long will my battery last?
Runtime = (Capacity kWh × DoD) ÷ Load kW. A 10kWh lithium battery at 80% DoD powering 2kW of appliances lasts about 4 hours.
Can I add more panels later?
Yes, if your inverter's MPPT input allows it. Check the max PV input wattage on your inverter spec sheet before expanding your array.
What happens during a blackout?
A hybrid inverter automatically switches to battery and solar power within milliseconds. Your loads continue without interruption as long as the battery has charge.
How do I read my system size?
System size is quoted as: solar (kWp) + battery (kWh) + inverter (kW). e.g. "5kWp · 10kWh · 5kW hybrid" means 5kWp panels, 10kWh storage, 5kW inverter.
